Still Alive

It always seems as if the things you enjoy doing are the first things to fall to the wayside when things get even slightly hectic in life. It’s been a while since I’ve sat down to work on the site directly despite thinking about it almost every day.

So, what’s new? Well, the biggest thing would be that I got a new motorcycle! My previous one, a Kawasaki Z900 developed a crack in the gas tank over the winter and it would have been over 2 grand to fix. Considering that is over a quarter what I bought her for, I just couldn’t bring myself to pay for it.

So now I own a 2025 Triumph Speed Twin 900. Technically a downgrade from the Z900, having almost half the horsepower of the old bike (63hp vs 108) but it’s just a much more enjoyable ride. Being a café style bike it’s much more upright of a riding style, for which my back thanks me. Incredible torque off the line as well. When I took it for a test ride I swear I nearly slipped off the bike coming out the parking lot. Zenia had speed… this one has spunk. Oh, and I found a pair of adorable canvas saddlebags for it! You’d think they were stock for how well they fit!

Beyond that, on the 4th of July the partner and I went to the Fredrick Meijer Garden in Grand Rapids to see their Chihuly glass scultpure exhibit. Apparently Dale Chihuly was the first American to ever work in the Venini glass facotry in Murano. If you ever get the chance to check out his scultpures, I highly recommend it!

Finished a couple of sewing projects since my last update. Did a nice jacket for the partner and made a pair of super flowy pants for myself! Maybe one day I’ll feel brave enough to start sharing pictures of my projects. Maybe I can get a mannequin to dress up! That’d be fun!

I know I promised the second part of the code breakdown as well, which I’ll try my best to get up before much longer. I’ll eventually want to do something similar for foxHound but… that one will take a lot longer.

Anyway, not much else to say, I suppose. Maybe if I actually put these out a bit more often I’d be able to make them a bit longer with actual topics to discuss. Until next time! Oh, and don’t forget. Make a neat gun.
