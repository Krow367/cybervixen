Welcome to the site!

So... If you're new here, you may be wondering what's going on and what's with these commands? Why the hell don't I have links and menus like any other self-respecting web dev? Well, there's a couple of reasons for that.

For one, my earliest computer usage was the old MSDOS command line operating system. I've always been enamored with the older computers and what developers were able to do with them despite their limitations.

For two... well, this site is a game of sorts. It's an ongoing project of mine that I hope will span years worth of development. If you haven't figured it out just yet, use the Repair command to get started. The first goal will become pretty obvious from there. After that, well, you'll get hints. Some of them will be /well labeled/ and give clues as to what commands you'll need to progress. If you do happen to reach the "end" of the current development of the story, don't worry, I will always make that clear and unambiguous.

I will say, however, that the "blog" and "recipes" will NEVER be a part of the game. Anything you read there is purely real-world only. Anything else is fair game, though.
Guess you'll just have to explore everything. ~.^

Why not just make a game?

Honestly... I dunno. I've always enjoyed ARGs and the RPGs and roleplay and such. Rogue-likes have always held a special place in my heart as well, especially the oldschool ones that use ASCII graphics. Cogmind, Dwarf Fortress, yadda yadda. After I discovered the indie web, I got the urge to create my own site. I didn't really know what I wanted to do with it, I just know I wanted one. Hell, I didn't even know what I wanted it to look like! But then I ran across another indie site. In the background was a game. Minesweeper. (If you know the one I'm talking about, Hi! If not... go check my links.) Anyway, for some reason, I thought that was really cool. That's what I wanted. A site that wasn't just a site, but a game as well. A site that was an *experience*. And since I can't draw for shit... ASCII saves the day! Eventually I ran across another site that had a cool scanline effect in the background like the old CRT monitors and... well, a short time later I was building the first version of the foxOS terminal!
