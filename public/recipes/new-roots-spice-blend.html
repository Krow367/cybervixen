Creole Salt


My partner was originally born in Louisiana and was pretty much raised by creole people. As a consequence, creole food has a very special place in their heart. When we moved in together, they created these two spice blends to mark the start of our new lives.

The New Roots blend is considered a creole salt. As the name implies, its their version of, well, salt. Put it on anything and everything. While I'm apparently "allergic to flavor" due to my sad midwestern upbringing, I do enjoy using this blend on occasion. According to my partner though, not nearly enough.

Scroll below for the Sweet Home blend, a similar blend meant for sweeter dishes (and coffee!)


New Roots


  • 2 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp cayenna pepper
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp ground white pepper
  • 1 tsp lava salt
  • 1/2 tsp celery salt
  • 1/2 tsp MSG

A little note about the MSG: The hate for MSG is completely baseless. First off, it's in everything. Ever eat fast food? MSG. It's completely safe. So why does it have such a bad rep? Well, like most things in America... racism. In the 1970s and 1980s Asian restaurants, especially those disease-spreading all-you-can-eat buffets, became very popular in the US. People would binge eat to the point of excess and would feel like shit afterward. Headaches caused by dehydration of high sodium foods, stomach aches from too much eating, sluggishness from the same, etc. This became known as Chinese Restaurant Syndrome and the culprit? MSG. It was easier to point at this "exotic" ingredient as the cause of all our problems rather than admit we're fucking idiots that gorge ourselves to excess. There's never been any negative health risks associated with controlled MSG consumption (like anything, if you ate too much of it I'm sure there's something bad that would happen but.. you don't eat an entire jar of salt at once, do you?).

If you're still afraid of MSG, leave it out. But it's a fantastic ingredient that not only contains less overall sodium than table salt (good), but it really does bring out other flavors better (very good). Give it a try. If you live in America you've already been eating it in processed food for years now anyway.

Sweet Home


Like the New Roots blend above, Sweet Home was created by my partner when they first moved in. They frequently add this blend to the grounds of their coffee and it really does add a wonderful spicey kick to it! While this one wouldn't be everyone's cup of tea (heh) I recommend you at least give it a shot. It's also surprisingly good with vanilla icecream as well. Be advenderous!

One note before the recipe itself, is the vanilla powder. I have personally never seen this in stores. While you can find it online, I would personally recommend getting a mortar and pestle and grinding it yourself from the beans. A mortar and pestle is very easy to learn how to use and is quite handy to have! I've used it to grind everything from home dried herbs to even making salsa with mine! The only thing you need to make sure of is to get one made of granite or marble (I think granite gives a better grind). General stoneware ones are not nearly as good, and a $20 granite set will outlast your great grandchildren. Just don't drop it on your toes. You won't win that fight. You can guess how I know.
