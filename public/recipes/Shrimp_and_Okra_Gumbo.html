Shrimp and Okra Gumbo

Click here for printer friendly version!

If you’ve never had the pleasure of gumbo before, then you are definitely in for a treat. Originating from the creoles of Louisiana, gumbo is a hearty stew with strong seasonings and a powerful flavor. The key comes from the “holy trinity” of creole cooking; celery, bell pepper, and onion, all cooked down into a roux and thickened by the okra. While this recipe is technically a two-parter, you can still start the main portion in the morning before work and cook more than 4 hours. Just be sure to give the shrimp and okra a full hour to properly cook before serving. This is one of the few dishes that actually benefits from an even longer cook time! The longer it goes, the more the roux can break down properly. 4 hours is merely the minimum. Remember, with gumbo, if you have to “chew” the vegetables, it’s not done yet!

Serve it over fresh rice and you have a delicious, hearty meal that is as perfect for those cold winter nights as it is for the breezy summer evenings.

Note: This recipe is for a slow cooker!

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces kielbasa sausages, halved lengthwise and cut into 1/4-inch halfmoons

  • 3 celery stalks, 1/4-inch slices

  • 6 green onions

  • 8 cloves garlic, minced

  • 1 cup chicken broth

  • 14 oz diced tomatoes

  • 1 pound large raw deveined tailless shrimp (if you struggle to find tailless, tail on will - work but I find it more annoying to eat)

  • 2 cups cut okra (if using frozen, let it fully thaw first, otherwise you will drag the temperature down and it won’t cook right! Same goes for the shrimp above)

  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped

  • 1 medium white or sweet onion, chopped

  • 1 tbsp olive oil

  • 2 tsp Cajun seasoning (hint, our New Roots blend is a fantastic choice here over store bought!)

  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme (I prefer to dry my own and lightly crush whole leaves, but ground thyme works almost as well.)

Directions

  1. Heat oil in skillet over medium-high heat. Add Kielbasa and brown (~four minutes). Add to crock pop.

  2. Add bell pepper, chopped onion, celery, green onions and garlic, cook for ~6 minutes until veggies are crisp-tender. Add to crock pot.

  3. In crock pot, stir in broth, tomatoes, seasoning, and thyme.

  4. Cover; cook on LOW 4 hours. Stir in shrimp and okra. Cover; cook on LOW 1 hour.
