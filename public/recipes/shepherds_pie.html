Shepherd's Pie


Click here for Printer Friendly Version!

While a typical shepherd's pie contains mutton, the one my mother always made never did. We've always made this dish in a 2.5 qt CorningWare dish. While the quality of the modern stuff isn't nearly as good as it used to be (they stopped using Pyroceram decades ago because it was too durable and was hurting sales because it just doesn't break) any similarly sized dish should be just fine. But if you make a lot of casserole type dishes? They're something to consider. And if you can get a cheap vintage set? Jump on that right away. You'll have cookware that your grandkids will use. Anyway. Here's the recipe.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 2 tbsp garlic powder (this is what it originally says, but I love garlic so I use way more. Measure with your heart.)
  • 2 cans condensed cream of mushroom soup
  • 1 can drained cut green beans
  • 24oz mashed potatoes (we use the pre-mashed storebought stuff but there's no reason you can't mash them yourself)
  • 2 cups shredded cheese (I like colby jack)


    • Directions

    1. If using pre-made, heat them up according to directions. Preheat oven to 350°F
    2. Brown ground beef in skillet
    3. Mix the beef, cream of mushrooms and green beans in round casserole dish
    4. Top with mashed potatoes, leaving a hole in the center
    6. Bake in oven for 30 minutes
    7. Remove from oven and top with shredded cheese, then bake for another 5-10 minutes until cheese is melted
    8. Serve and enjoy!
